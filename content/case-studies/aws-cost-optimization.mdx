## The Challenge

An enterprise client was facing spiraling AWS costs with a monthly bill of $24,000 and no clear visibility into what was driving the spend. Resources were provisioned without tagging, many instances were idle or over-provisioned, and there was no cost governance in place.

## The Approach

Conducted a full audit of the multi-service AWS environment, analyzing compute, storage, database, and networking resources across all accounts and regions.

## The Solution

- Identified and eliminated idle compute resources and orphaned storage volumes
- Right-sized over-provisioned EC2 and RDS instances
- Implemented reserved instances and savings plans for steady-state workloads
- Set up storage tiering with lifecycle policies for S3 data
- Applied consistent tagging for cost allocation and tracking
- Configured budget alerts and automated scaling policies

## Results

- **Monthly bill dropped from $24,000 to $6,000** — a 75% reduction
- **$216,000 saved annually**
- Improved performance through right-sized resources
- Automated cost governance going forward
